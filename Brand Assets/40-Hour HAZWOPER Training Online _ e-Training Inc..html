What Is eTraining’s 40-Hour HAZWOPER Training Course?

eTraining's 40-Hour HAZWOPER (Hazardous Waste Operations and Emergency Response) training course is an OSHA-authorized online training program developed to meet the requirements of OSHA 29 CFR 1910.120. The course is designed for workers and employees who may be exposed to hazardous substances, hazardous materials, or hazardous waste at their workplace — including those involved in uncontrolled hazardous waste cleanup operations, treatment, storage and disposal activities, emergency response operations, and corrective action sites regulated under RCRA.

The training is delivered 100% online across 10 modules and covers hazard identification, PPE, decontamination, emergency response, site characterization and control, and other OSHA HAZWOPER requirements. Per OSHA 29 CFR 1910.120(e)(3)(i), completion of this online course must be followed by three days of employer-provided, supervised, site-specific field training conducted by an experienced supervisor.

Who Should Take 40-Hour HAZWOPER Training?

This course is required for workers who may be exposed to hazardous substances on the job.

  • Workers at uncontrolled hazardous waste cleanup sites
  • Employees at RCRA corrective action sites
  • Workers at hazardous waste treatment, storage, and disposal (TSD) facilities
  • Emergency response personnel handling hazardous substance releases
  • Environmental remediation workers
  • Hazardous materials (HazMat) technicians
  • Supervisors and site managers overseeing hazardous waste operations
  • New workers who have never completed HAZWOPER training

What Industries Require 40-Hour HAZWOPER Training?

HAZWOPER training is required across a wide range of industries that handle or respond to hazardous materials.

  • Environmental remediation and cleanup contractors
  • Hazardous waste treatment, storage, and disposal facilities
  • Chemical manufacturing and processing plants
  • Oil and gas operations (upstream, midstream, downstream)
  • Nuclear and radiological waste management
  • Mining and mineral extraction operations
  • Emergency response teams and HazMat units
  • Government agencies (EPA, DOE, military installations)
  • Utility companies dealing with hazardous materials
  • Transportation companies hauling hazardous cargo
