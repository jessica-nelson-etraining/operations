Frequently Asked Questions About
40-Hour HAZWOPER Training

Common questions about HAZWOPER certification cover who needs it, how long it takes, what OSHA requires, whether online training is accepted, and how to maintain it with the annual 8-hour refresher.

40-Hour HAZWOPER training is an OSHA-required safety course for workers who may be exposed to hazardous substances or hazardous waste. Required by OSHA standard 29 CFR 1910.120, it covers hazard identification, personal protective equipment, decontamination, emergency response procedures, and safe work practices at hazardous waste sites. It is the foundational HAZWOPER training level for general site workers.

OSHA requires 40-Hour HAZWOPER training for general site workers at uncontrolled hazardous waste operations, workers at RCRA corrective action sites, employees at treatment/storage/disposal (TSD) facilities, emergency response personnel, and supervisors managing hazardous waste operations. Workers with limited exposure who are not involved in direct cleanup may qualify for the 24-Hour HAZWOPER course instead.

The course requires 40 hours of instruction across 10 modules. Because it is self-paced, you can complete it over several days or weeks at your own schedule. Most students finish in 1–2 weeks of focused study. Progress saves automatically so you can pause and resume on any device at any time.

40-Hour HAZWOPER is for general site workers with the highest potential exposure to hazardous substances. 24-Hour HAZWOPER is for workers with limited exposure who are not involved in direct cleanup operations. 8-Hour HAZWOPER Refresher is the annual requirement for anyone who has already completed 24-hour or 40-hour training — required to maintain certification every 12 months.

Yes. HAZWOPER certification is valid for 12 months from the date of completion. OSHA requires all HAZWOPER-trained workers to complete an 8-Hour HAZWOPER Refresher course annually to maintain their certification. If you miss the annual refresher deadline, you may be required to retake the full 40-Hour course.

Yes. Online HAZWOPER training is a valid option recognized by OSHA for meeting the instructional requirements under 29 CFR 1910.120. OSHA also requires 3 days of supervised, hands-on field experience provided by the employer following online training completion. The online course fulfills the classroom instruction portion of the OSHA requirement.

Per OSHA 29 CFR 1910.120(e)(3)(i), employers must provide 3 days (24 hours) of supervised field experience at the actual work site following completion of the 40-Hour HAZWOPER online course. This must be conducted under an experienced supervisor and cover site-specific hazards, PPE use, and emergency procedures relevant to your workplace.

The course covers 10 modules: (1) Introduction to HAZWOPER & OSHA regulations, (2) Site Characterization and Control, (3) Hazardous Substance Properties, (4) Toxicology and Health Effects, (5) Personal Protective Equipment (PPE), (6) Respiratory Protection & NIOSH Standards, (7) Decontamination Procedures, (8) Emergency Response Planning, (9) Confined Space Entry at HAZWOPER Sites, and (10) Monitoring Procedures & Equipment.
