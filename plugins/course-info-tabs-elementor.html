Who Should Take the 9-Hour HAZWOPER Refresher?

This course is required for any worker who has completed initial HAZWOPER certification and needs to maintain it.

  • Workers who have completed 40-hour or 24-hour HAZWOPER initial training
  • Hazardous waste cleanup and remediation workers
  • Emergency responders with HAZWOPER certification (HazMat technicians, on-scene commanders)
  • Environmental consultants and engineers on remediation projects
  • Industrial facility workers handling or working near hazardous materials
  • Oil and gas workers at upstream, midstream, and downstream operations
  • Construction workers on sites with known or suspected contamination
  • Government employees (EPA, DOE, military installations) with HAZWOPER credentials
  • Any worker whose HAZWOPER certification is approaching its 12-month renewal date

Industries That Require Annual HAZWOPER Refresher Training

HAZWOPER annual refresher training is required across all industries where initial HAZWOPER certification was obtained.

  • Environmental remediation and hazardous waste cleanup contractors
  • Chemical manufacturing and processing plants
  • Oil and gas — upstream, midstream, and downstream operations
  • Hazardous waste treatment, storage, and disposal (TSD) facilities
  • Emergency response and HazMat units (fire departments, industrial brigades)
  • Mining and mineral extraction operations
  • Utility companies managing hazardous materials or waste streams
  • Transportation companies hauling hazardous cargo (per DOT/OSHA overlap)
  • Nuclear and radiological waste management facilities
  • Government agencies — EPA Superfund sites, DOE installations, military bases
  • Construction contractors on brownfield or contaminated site projects
